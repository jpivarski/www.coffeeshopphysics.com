Title: The dark matter we know

Today, the word “dark matter” is used to describe an exotic type of matter, and many physicists hope to discover it in the laboratory, to learn what its properties are. When the term was coined, however, it simply meant any astronomical mass that doesn't glow as a star.  By that definition, we are dark matter.

The first robust indications that the non-glowing matter in the universe was vastly underestimated came in the form of galaxy rotation curves
